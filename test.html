The Climb

By Edward Jones

Every dawn begins unseen,
Behind the clouds, beyond the green.
Small footsteps shape the largest road,
Strong hearts lighten every load.

Dreams may stumble, hopes may bend,
Yet faithful hands still learn and mend.
The stars applaud the ones who try,
Not those afraid to reach the sky.

So rise again when days are long,
Let every failure make you strong.
For mountains bow to those who climb,
One faithful step, one at a time.
